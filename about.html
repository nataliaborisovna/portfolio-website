    Inspiration

    Jared Spool is valued for his expertise in usability and his ability to communicate complex design concepts in a clear and accessible manner.

    "Good design, when it's done well, becomes invisible. It's only when it's done poorly that we notice it."

    His quote underscores the goal of design to seamlessly integrate into the user experience, enhancing usability and satisfaction without drawing attention to itself.

    Don Norman is admired for his influential work in HCI and his emphasis on the importance of understanding users in the design process.

    "Design is really an act of communication, which means having a deep understanding of the person with whom the designer is communicating."

    His quote highlights the central role of empathy and communication in creating effective and meaningful design solutions.

    Alan Cooper inspired me with his advocacy of user-centered design and his efforts to promote empathy and understanding in the design process. He is commited to challenging barriers to knowledge and fostering a deeper understanding of user needs.

    Sara Wachter-Boettcher has my respect and admiration for her critical examination of the ethical implications of technology design. Throughout her work, she highlights the importance of recognizing the biases and assumptions embedded in technology and the need for designers to consider the broader social and ethical implications.

    Brenda Laurel is known for her pioneering work in interactive storytelling and her exploration of the intersection between technology and narrative. She believed in the power of storytelling to shape our understanding of the world and the role of technology in facilitating human expression and communication.
